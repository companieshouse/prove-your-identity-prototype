Verify your identity for Companies House

How to verify your identity so that you can set up, run, own or file for a company registered at Companies House.

Contents

  1. Why you need to verify your identity
  2. Who needs to verify their identity
  3. What happens when you verify
  4. When you need to verify
  5. What happens if you do not verify

Why you need to verify your identity

You need to verify your identity to confirm you are who you say you are.

This will:

  • reduce the risk of fraud
  • improve the accuracy of information on the Companies House register

Who needs to verify their identity

You need to verify your identity if you are:

  • a person with significant control (PSC)
  • a director
  • the equivalent of a director – this includes members, general partners and managing officers
  • someone who files for a company, but is not supervised under the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regulations.

What happens when you verify

You only need to verify your identity once. After you’ve verified, we’ll give you a personal verification code.

You’ll need to provide this:

  • as part of some other company filings
  • when you are appointed as a director
  • when you are appointed as a PSC

Only share the code with people you trust to file on your behalf.

When you need to verify

New companies

All directors must verify their identity when incorporating (starting) a company. PSCs have 14 days from the day the company was incorporated to verify.

Current directors of existing companies

Verify your identity as soon as possible, but no later than the due date of the next confirmation statement.

All current directors of existing companies must verify their identity. The company must then file a verification statement with its next confirmation statement, after all directors have verified.

New directors of existing companies

You must verify your identity before you notify (tell) Companies House you’ve become a director.

Current PSCs of existing companies

Verify your identity as soon as possible, but no later than 14 days after the due date of the next confirmation statement. Search for the company on the Companies House register to find the date.

Relevant legal entities (RLEs) must identify a relevant officer (the equivalent of a director). As a relevant officer, you must verify your identity as soon as possible, but no later than 28 days after the due date of the next confirmation statement.

New PSCs of existing companies

Verify your identity as soon as possible, but no later than 14 days after the company gives notice to Companies House.

Relevant legal entities (RLEs) must identify a relevant officer (the equivalent of a director). As a relevant officer, you must verify your identity as soon as possible, but no later than 28 days after the company gives notice to Companies House.

If you file at Companies House

From [date], you must verify your identity before you make your next filing at Companies House.

When to verify if you have multiple roles in a company
For directors, verify your identity as soon as possible, but no later than the due date of the next confirmation statement.
For PSCs, verify your identity as soon as possible, but no later than 14 days after the due date of the next confirmation statement.
When to verify if you have multiple companies
Verify your identity as soon as possible, but no later than the date when the earliest confirmation statement is due. Search for the companies on the Companies House register to find the dates.

What happens if you do not verify

There will be a transition period until [date] so that you have time to do what is required by the regulations.

If you do not verify your identity in time, you are committing an offence. The public register will show that you are not verified.

You could also:

  • get a maximum fine of £XXX
  • be stopped from acting as a director
  • be unable to make any filings for your company
  • be unable to start a new company
