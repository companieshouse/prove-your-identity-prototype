Ways you can verify your identity for Companies House

Based on your answers, here are the ways you can verify your identity.

Use an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP)

You can ask an ACSP to verify your identity on your behalf. For example, an accountant or solicitor. This is also known as a Companies House authorised agent.

You can do this from any country, but your agent must register with Companies House in order to become authorised. You’ll need to provide suitable identity documents.

It can be quicker to verify your identity using an ACSP.

If you do not want to use an ACSP

You can provide evidence of your identity to Companies House instead.

You'll need to:

  • provide your identity documents
  • authenticate your identity using facial recognition
! Warning If you choose this option, it could take up to 10 working days before we email you with information about how to verify.
You may be able to try and verify using GOV.UK One Login again
If you do not currently have a passport, but are able to get one, you may be able to try and verify using GOV.UK One Login again (opens in new tab).
What happens if you do not verify your identity

There will be a transition period for existing directors and people with significant control (PSCs), so that you have time to do what the regulations require. Find out when you need to verify your identity (opens in new tab).

{{ govukWarningText({ text: "If you do not comply with identity verification requirements on time, you may be committing an offence and may have to pay a financial penalty or fine.", iconFallbackText: "Warning" }) }}

You will also be unable to:

  • make any filings for your company or entity
  • start a new company or entity
  • register as a Companies House authorised agent

Directors

If you continue to act as a director after the deadline without verifying, you will be committing an offence. The company (or entity) and all directors may also be committing an offence.

People with significant control (PSCs)

If you continue to be a PSC after the deadline without verifying, you may be committing an offence.
