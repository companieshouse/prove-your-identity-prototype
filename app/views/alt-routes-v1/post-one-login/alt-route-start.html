Use vouching and evidence to verify your identity for Companies House

How to verify your identity by asking someone to vouch for you, and providing evidence.

What evidence you’ll need

Identity documents

You will need to provide 2 documents in total. You must provide at least one photographic document.

You can provide either:

  • 2 documents from group A
  • 1 document from group A, and 1 document from group B

Group A

You can use:

  • passport
  • driving licence
  • [other types of ID]

Group B

You can use:

  • a utility bill
  • a mortgage statement
  • [other types of documents]

Translations

If any of the documents you upload are written in a language other than English, you’ll also need to upload a translation of the document.

In names that use non-English characters, you’ll need to provide a transliteration. This means converting them from the original text into the equivalent sounds and letters in the English alphabet. For example, the Greek name "Ελένη" is transliterated as ‘Eleni’ in English.

We can accept diacritics (signs above or below letters that change the pronunciation). For example, an accent above the letter é, or a cedilla below the letter ç.

You will need to declare that you certify that the original document has been translated.

Video

You’ll need to record a video of yourself. We’ll provide instructions for how to do this in this service.

When you record the video, make sure you are:

  • in a well-lit room
  • not using any background effects

After you've uploaded all your evidence, you'll then need to ask someone to vouch for you.

What vouching is

Vouching is when a person declares they know another person, and that they are who they say they are.

The person who is doing the vouching is known as a 'voucher'.

Who can vouch for your identity

Someone can vouch for you if they meet the following conditions.

They must:

  • be in a position of authority in their community - for example, a teacher, doctor, solicitor or company director
  • have known you for X years
  • have verified their own identity using GOV.UK One Login
  • be based in the UK at the time of vouching
  • be able to vouch that you look like who you claim to be

They must not:

  • be related to you by birth or marriage, or another family relationship (such as adoption)
  • be in a relationship with you
  • live with you

Check the complete list of people who can vouch for someone's identity. It is your responsibility to find someone who will vouch for you.

What happens next

After you've uploaded your evidence and it's been accepted, and your voucher has done XYZ [whatever the voucher needs to do], we'll email your Companies House personal code to you.

What a Companies House personal code is

This is an 11 character code that is given to a person once they've verified their identity. The code is personal to you, not your company or a company you work for.

You'll need your personal code for various reasons. For example:

  • as part of some company filings, such as the confirmation statement
  • if you are appointed as a director
  • if you become a person with significant control (PSC)
