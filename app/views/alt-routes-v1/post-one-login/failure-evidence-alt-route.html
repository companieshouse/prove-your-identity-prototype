ACSP

You'll soon be able to ask a Companies House authorised agent (such as an accountant or solicitor) to verify your identity on your behalf. This is also known as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP). You can do this from any country, but your agent must register with Companies House in order to become authorised. They must be registered with a UK Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body. You’ll need to provide suitable identity documents.

What happens if you do not verify

There will be a transition period for existing directors and people with significant control (PSCs), so that you have time to do what the regulations require. Find out when you need to verify your identity.

{{ govukWarningText({ text: "If you do not comply with identity verification requirements on time, you will be committing an offence and may have to pay a financial penalty or fine.", iconFallbackText: "Warning" }) }}

You will also be unable to:

  • make any filings for your company or entity
  • start a new company or entity
  • register as a Companies House authorised agent

Directors

If you continue to act as a director after the deadline without verifying, you will be committing an offence. The company (or entity) and all directors may also be committing an offence.

People with significant control (PSCs)

If you continue to be a PSC after the deadline without verifying, you will be committing an offence.
