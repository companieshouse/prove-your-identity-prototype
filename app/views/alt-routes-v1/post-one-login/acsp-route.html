How to verify using an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP)

You will need to find an ACSP to verify your identity. This is also known as a Companies House authorised agent.

You can do this from any country, but your agent must register with Companies House in order to become authorised.

Who can be an ACSP

An ACSP is a person or organisation that is registered with a UK Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body. For example:

  • company formation agents
  • accountants
  • solicitors

How to find an ACSP

If you have an existing accountant or solicitor (or any other AML-supervised professional), you can ask them if they are registered with Companies House as an ACSP. If not, you'll need to find one. For example, by doing an online search.

What happens next

When an ACSP has agreed to verify your identity, you will need to provide documents from an approved list as evidence of your identity. They may charge a fee for their services.
