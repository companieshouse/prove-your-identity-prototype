Verify with a Companies House authorised agent

Ask an authorised agent (such as an accountant or solicitor) to verify your identity with Companies House on your behalf.

Your identity will only be verified for Companies House, not any other government service or department.

If your agent is not yet authorised

Agents must register with Companies House in order to become authorised.

Who can be an authorised agent

The agent must be registered in the UK with Companies House as an authorised agent - also known as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP). They must be registered with an Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body.

They could be:

  • accountants
  • solicitors
  • company formation agents

What happens next

When your authorised agent has agreed to verify your identity on your behalf, they will ask you to provide proof of your address and identity.

Find out which documents you can use to prove your identity in the UK.
