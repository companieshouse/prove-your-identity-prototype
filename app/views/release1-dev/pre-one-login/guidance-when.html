Guidance

When you need to verify your identity

There will be a transition period for existing directors and people with significant control (PSCs), so that you have time to do what the regulations require.

Sole traders

If you want to apply to register as a Companies House authorised agent, you must verify your identity before you apply. These are also known as Authorised Corporate Service Providers, or ACSPs, and usually have roles such as accountants, solicitors or company formation agents.

Directors

Directors are not required to verify their identity yet. When this becomes a requirement, the following guidance explains when you’ll need to verify.

Current directors of existing companies

When it becomes a requirement, you’ll need to verify no later than the due date of the next confirmation statement.

New directors of existing companies

When it becomes a requirement, you’ll need to verify before you notify (tell) Companies House you’ve become a director.

Directors of new companies

When it becomes a requirement, all directors will need to verify their identity before incorporating (starting) a company.

People with significant control (PSCs)

PSCs are not required to verify their identity yet. When this becomes a requirement, the following guidance explains when you’ll need to verify.

Existing companies are companies that exist on the day identity verification for directors and PSCs comes into force.

Current PSCs of existing companies

When it becomes a requirement, you'll need to verify no later than 14 days after the due date of the next confirmation statement. You can find the due date when you search for the company on the Companies House register.

New PSCs of existing companies

When it becomes a requirement, you’ll need to verify no later than 14 days after the company gives notice to Companies House.

PSCs of new companies

When it becomes a requirement, you'll have 14 days from the day the company was incorporated to verify.

Relevant legal entities (RLEs)

RLEs are not required to verify their identity yet. When this becomes a requirement, the following guidance explains when you’ll need to verify.

Current RLEs of existing companies

Relevant legal entities (RLEs) must identify a relevant officer (the equivalent of a director). As a relevant officer, when it becomes a requirement, you'll need to verify no later than 28 days after the due date of the next confirmation statement.

New RLEs of existing companies

Relevant legal entities (RLEs) must identify a relevant officer (the equivalent of a director). As a relevant officer, when it becomes a requirement, you'll need to verify no later than 28 days after the company gives notice to Companies House.

RLEs of new companies

When it becomes a requirement, you'll have 28 days from the day the company was incorporated to verify.

People who file at Companies House

People who file are not required to verify their identity yet. When this becomes a requirement, the following guidance explains when you’ll need to verify.

When it becomes a requirement, you'll need to verify before you make your next filing at Companies House.

People who have multiple roles in a company

Directors and PSCs are not required to verify their identity yet. When this becomes a requirement, the following guidance explains when you’ll need to verify.

If you are a director and a PSC, when it becomes a requirement, you'll need to verify no later than the due date of the next confirmation statement.

People who have multiple companies

People who are directors or PSCs of multiple companies are not required to verify their identity yet. When this becomes a requirement, the following guidance explains when you’ll need to verify.

When it becomes a requirement, you'll need to verify no later than the date when the earliest confirmation statement is due. You can find the due date when you search for the company on the Companies House register.
