Verify your identity for Companies House

You need to prove who you are to set up, run, own or file for a company registered at Companies House.

Why you need to verify your identity

You need to verify your identity to confirm you are who you say you are.

This will:

  • reduce the risk of fraud
  • improve the accuracy of information on the Companies House register

What happens if you do not verify

There will be a transition period so that you have time to do what the regulations require. Find out when you need to verify your identity.

{{ govukWarningText({ text: "If you do not verify your identity in time, you could get a maximum fine of £XXX.", iconFallbackText: "Warning" }) }}

You will also be unable to:

  • make any filings for your company or entity
  • start a new company or entity
  • register as a Companies House authorised agent

If you continue to act as a director after the deadline, you will be committing an offence. The company (or entity) and all directors may also be committing an offence.

Who needs to verify their identity

You only need to verify your identity once. You need to verify your identity if you are:

  • a person with significant control (PSC)
  • a director
  • the equivalent of a director – this includes members, general partners and managing officers
  • someone who files for a company

All companies registered with Companies House will receive an email or a letter (or both) asking relevant people to verify their identity.

Ways to verify your identity

Online

You can use the Verify your identity for Companies House service to verify online if you have the required identity documents.

You'll need one of the following types of photo ID:

  • biometric passport from any country
  • UK photo driving licence (full or provisional)
  • UK biometric residence permit (BRP)

In person

If you do not have the required identity documents, but you live in the UK, you can use the Verify your identity for Companies House service to find out how to verify in person at the Post Office.

You'll need one of the following types of photo ID:

  • European Union (EU) photo driving licence
  • non-UK passport
  • national identity card from a European Economic Area (EEA) country

Using a Companies House authorised agent

You'll soon be able to ask a Companies House authorised agent (such as an accountant or solicitor) to verify your identity on your behalf. You can do this from any country, but your agent must register with Companies House in order to become authorised. You’ll need to provide suitable identity documents.

What happens when you verify

When you have successfully verified, you'll be given a Companies House personal code. You'll need to use your code to connect your verified identity to our records. This is so that we know the correct person is linked to the name we have on file. The code is personal to you, not your company or a company you work for.

You'll need your personal code for various reasons. For example:

  • as part of some other company filings, such as the confirmation statement
  • if you are appointed as a director
  • if you become a person with significant control (PSC)

Only share the code with people you trust

You may need to share this code with people you trust to file on your behalf, or for your company. Keep this information secure, as you would with other unique codes such as your Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) for HMRC.

Identity verification makes it much more difficult for criminals to impersonate someone, but not impossible.
