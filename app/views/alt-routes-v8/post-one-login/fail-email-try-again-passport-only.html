Email subject line: Important: Identity verification for Companies House outcome and what you need to do next


Important: Identity verification outcome and what you need to do next

Your case reference number: HW123456

We have reviewed the information you submitted when you applied to verify your identity.

Outcome: Unsuccessful

Based on the information you supplied, you have not been able to prove who you are to Companies House. For security reasons, we cannot tell you the reason why.

Next steps

You have 2 options to try and verify your identity.

Option 1

You can try and verify again using the 'Provide evidence of your identity to Companies House' service. You must do this within 10 days.

If you do not submit your identity information within 10 days

If you miss the deadline, we will close your case. You will have to go back to the beginning of the ‘Verify your identity for Companies House’ service and start again. It will take longer to become verified, and you may not be able to file statutory documents in time.

What you'll need

You'll need to provide 2 documents to verify your identity, and a device with a camera (a smartphone or a tablet). You can ask someone, such as a friend or family member, to help you at any stage of the process if you need to.

If you provide a document type that's not listed in this email, your application to verify your identity will be rejected.

1. Identity document

This must be your [nationality] passport. It can be expired by up to 18 months.

2. Supporting document

This must be one of the following:

  • mortgage statement or rental agreement
  • utility bill
  • bank or building society statement
  • insurance policy document
  • employment record

Your supporting document must be more than 6 months old. It shows us that your identity has existed over time. If your document is less than 6 months old, your application to verify your identity will be rejected.

It must also:

  • show your name as it appears in your identity document
  • show the date of your document
  • be uploaded as an image (.jpeg or .png)

How to prepare your documents to upload

The image must:

  • be in colour
  • be clear, in focus, and easy to read
  • be taken in a well-lit room
  • not be altered by computer software
  • not be cut off or cropped
  • not be taken at an angle

The document must:

  • be laid flat against a plain background - if you hold the document in your hand, make sure no information is hidden
  • be the original document – we cannot accept a screenshot, or a photo taken of the document on a screen
  • not be repaired or damaged, including water damage, creases or folds
  • not be presented in a decorative or clear cover

Make sure:

  • the expiry date of your identity document is within the accepted limits
  • you follow all on screen instructions carefully to make sure your documents and face are captured correctly
