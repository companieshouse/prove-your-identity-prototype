Verifying with an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP)

An ACSP can verify your identity for Companies House on your behalf. For example, an accountant or solicitor. This is also known as an authorised agent.

You can do this from any country, but your agent must register with Companies House in order to become authorised. They must be registered with a UK Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body.

If you use an agent, your identity will only be verified for Companies House, not any other government service or department.

How to find an ACSP

If you have an existing accountant or solicitor (or any other AML-supervised professional), you can ask them if they are registered with Companies House as an ACSP. If not, you can find one using the list of Authorised Corporate Service Providers (ACSPs) (opens in new tab).

What happens next

When an ACSP has agreed to verify your identity, you will need to provide documents from an approved list as evidence of your identity. The ACSP may charge a fee for their services.
