Verify your identity at Companies House

How to verify your identity so that you can set up, run, own or control a company registered at Companies House.

What this service is for

This service enables you to verify your identity directly with Companies House, to confirm you are who you claim to be.

Identity verification was introduced by the Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency (ECCT) Bill to help to increase transparency over UK companies and improve the accuracy of information on the Companies House register.

Who can use this service

You can use this service if you’re a:

  • director (or equivalent, such as a partner)
  • person with significant control (PSC)

For new companies and other registerable legal entities, all directors (or equivalent) and PSCs will need to verify their identity before or as part of the process of incorporating a company.

For existing companies on the register, all directors (or equivalent) will need to verify their identity by XX/XX/XX. All PSCs will need to verify their identity by XX/XX/XX.

What happens after you’ve verified your identity

We’ll give you a Verification ID. You’ll need to enter this the next time you, or someone else, files at Companies House for your company.

What happens if you do not verify your identity

If you do not verify your identity by the deadline, we'll annotate the public Companies House register to state that your identity has not been verified, and your company could be struck off the register.
