Verify your identity for Companies House

You need to prove who you are to set up, run, own or file for a company registered at Companies House.

Why you need to verify your identity

You need to verify your identity to confirm you are who you say you are.

This will:

  • reduce the risk of fraud
  • improve the accuracy of information on the Companies House register

What happens if you do not verify

There will be a transition period until [date] so that you have time to do what is required by the regulations.

You will also be unable to:

  • make any filings for your company or entity
  • start a new company or entity
  • register as a Companies House authorised agent

If you continue to act as a director after the deadline, you will be committing an offence. The company (or entity) and all directors may also be committing an offence.

Who needs to verify their identity

You only need to verify your identity once. You need to verify your identity if you are:

  • a person with significant control (PSC)
  • a director
  • the equivalent of a director – this includes members, general partners and managing officers
  • someone who files for a company

All companies registered with Companies House will receive an email or a letter (or both) asking relevant people to verify their identity.

Ways to verify your identity

Online

You can use the Verify your identity for Companies House service to verify online if you have the required identity documents.

You'll need a (one of the following):

  • biometric passport from any country
  • UK photo driving licence (full or provisional)
  • UK biometric residence permit (BRP)

In person

If you do not have the required identity documents, but you live in the UK, you can use the Verify your identity for Companies House service to find out how to verify in person at the Post Office.

Using a Companies House authorised agent

You can ask a Companies House authorised agent (such as an accountant or solicitor) to verify your identity on your behalf. You can do this from any country, but your agent must be registered in the UK.

More about verifying with a Companies House authorised agent.

What happens when you verify

When you have successfully verified, we’ll send you a Companies House personal code that connects your verified identity to our records. This is so that we know the correct person is linked to the name we have on file.

You may need your Companies House personal code:

  • as part of some other company filings
  • when you are appointed as a director
  • when you are appointed as a PSC

Keeping your information secure

Identity verification makes it much more difficult for criminals to impersonate someone, but not impossible. Keep this information secure, as you would with other unique codes such as your Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) for HMRC. Only share the code with people you trust to file on your behalf.
