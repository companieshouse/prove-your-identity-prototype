Verifying your identity with a Companies House authorised agent

An authorised agent can verify your identity for Companies House on your behalf. They must be registered with Companies House as an authorised agent - also known as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP). They must be registered with a UK Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body.

If you use an agent, your identity will only be verified for Companies House, not any other government service or department.

You can search for an agent on the list of Companies House authorised agents. They will charge a fee for their services.

Who can be an authorised agent

Authorised agents could be:

  • accountants
  • solicitors
  • company formation agents

What happens next

When an authorised agent has agreed to verify your identity, they will ask you to provide proof of your identity using documents from the Companies House identity verification standard.
