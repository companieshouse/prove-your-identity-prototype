Verify your identity with Companies House

How to verify your identity so that you can set up, run, own or control a company registered at Companies House.

Why you need to verify your identity

You need to verify your identity to confirm you are who you say you are.

Who needs to verify their identity

You need to verify your identity if you are:

  • a person with significant control (PSC)
  • a director
  • the equivalent of a director – this includes members, general partners and managing officers

What happens when you verify

After you’ve verified your identity, we’ll give you an identity code.

You’ll need to provide this:

  • as part of the verification statement
  • as part of some other company filings
  • when you are appointed as a director
  • when you are appointed as a PSC

What is a verification statement

This is a statement that confirms the directors and PSCs of a company are verified.

When you need to verify

New companies

All directors must verify their identity when incorporating (starting) a company. PSCs have 14 days from the day the company was incorporated to verify their identity and submit a verification statement.

Current directors of existing companies

Verify your identity as soon as possible, but no later than the due date of the next confirmation statement.

The company must file a verification statement with its next confirmation statement after all directors have verified their identity.

New directors of existing companies

You must verify your identity before you notify (tell) Companies House you’ve become a director.

Current PSCs of existing companies

Verify your identity and file a verification statement as soon as possible, but no later than 14 days after the due date of the next confirmation statement. Search for the company on the Companies House register to find the date.

New PSCs of existing companies

Verify your identity and file a verification statement as soon as possible, but no later than 14 days after the company gives notice to Companies House.

What happens if you do not verify

There will be a transition period until [date] so that you have time to do what is required by the regulations.

If you do not verify your identity in time, you are committing an offence. The public register will show that you are not verified.

You could also:

  • get a maximum fine of £XXX
  • be stopped from acting as a director
  • be unable to make any filings for your company
  • be unable to start a new company
