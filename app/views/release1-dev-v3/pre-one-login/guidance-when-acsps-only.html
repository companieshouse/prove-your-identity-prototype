Guidance

When you need to verify your identity for Companies House

When you need to verify depends on your role, and when you started that role. There will be a transition period for existing directors and people with significant control (PSCs), so that you have time to do what the regulations require.

Companies House authorised agents

You'll soon be able to verify your identity in order to register as a Companies House authorised agent.

If you want to apply to register as a Companies House authorised agent, you must verify your identity before you apply. These are also known as Authorised Corporate Service Providers (ACSPs), and usually have roles such as accountants, solicitors or company formation agents.

Directors

Directors are not required to verify their identity yet. We'll update this guidance with more information soon.

Current directors of existing companies

Current directors are directors that were appointed before [date].

When it becomes a requirement, you’ll need to verify no later than the due date of the next confirmation statement.

New directors of existing companies

New directors are directors that were appointed from [date].

When it becomes a requirement, you’ll need to verify before you notify (tell) Companies House you’ve become a director.

Directors of new companies

When it becomes a requirement, all directors will need to verify their identity before incorporating (starting) a company.

People with significant control (PSCs)

PSCs are not required to verify their identity yet. When this becomes a requirement, the following guidance explains when you’ll need to verify.

Existing companies are companies that were registered at Companies House before [date].

Current PSCs of existing companies

Current PSCs are PSCs that were registered with Companies House before [date].

When it becomes a requirement, you'll need to verify no later than 14 days after the date of the company's next confirmation statement. You can find the date when you search for the company on the Companies House register.

New PSCs of existing companies

New PSCs are PSCs that were registered at Companies House from [date].

When it becomes a requirement, you’ll need to verify no later than 14 days after the date they were registered at Companies House.

PSCs of new companies

When it becomes a requirement, you’ll need to verify no later than 14 days from the day the company was incorporated (started).

People who file at Companies House

People who file are not required to verify their identity yet. When this becomes a requirement, the following guidance explains when you’ll need to verify.

When it becomes a requirement, you'll need to verify before you submit your next filing at Companies House.

People who file are also known as presenters.

People who have multiple roles in a company

Directors and PSCs are not required to verify their identity yet. When this becomes a requirement, the following guidance explains when you’ll need to verify.

If you are a director and a PSC, when it becomes a requirement, you'll need to verify no later than the date of the next confirmation statement. In most cases you only need to verify your identity once, even if you are both a director and a PSC.

People who have multiple companies

People who are directors or PSCs of multiple companies are not required to verify their identity yet. When this becomes a requirement, the following guidance explains when you’ll need to verify.

When it becomes a requirement, you'll need to verify no later than the date of the earliest confirmation statement. You can find the date when you search for the company on the Companies House register. In most cases you only need to verify your identity once, even if you have multiple companies.
